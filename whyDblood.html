Benefits for the donor

Each person who donate blood complete a simple physical examination and blood test before giving blood.

These are not in-depth tests, but they may helps identify unknown concerns, such as anemia or high or low blood pressure

The test will check the person's

  • blood pressure
  • body temperature
  • heart rate
  • hemoglobin, or iron levels

If the test reveals a problem, the person will not be able to donate blood. However, the results could be a first step toward seeking treatment.
